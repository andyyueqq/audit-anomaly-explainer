# Period-End Close Procedures

**Policy Number:** FIN-CL-002
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Last Revised:** November 30, 2024
**Owner:** Assistant Controller

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the procedures and controls for the monthly and quarterly financial close process. It establishes deadlines, responsibilities, and restrictions on general-ledger activity after the close date.

## 2. Close Calendar

The financial close follows this standard timeline each month:

- **Day 1 of the following month:** Subledger close (AP, AR, Payroll). No new transactions may be entered in subledgers after this date without Assistant Controller approval.
- **Day 3 of the following month (Close Date):** General-ledger close. All standard journal entries, accruals, and reclassifications for the period must be posted by end of business on this date.
- **Day 5 of the following month:** Final review and sign-off by the Controller.
- **Day 10 of the following month:** Financial statements distributed to management.

For quarter-end and year-end, all deadlines are extended by 2 business days.

## 3. Post-Close Entry Restrictions

After the Close Date (Day 3), the general ledger is locked for the closed period. Any entry that must be posted after the Close Date is considered a **post-close adjustment** and is subject to the following restrictions:

### 3.1 Authorization

- Post-close entries require written approval from the Assistant Controller or Corporate Controller, regardless of the dollar amount.
- The request must include a written justification explaining why the entry was not posted before the Close Date.

### 3.2 Approved Post-Close Users

Only the following roles are authorized to post entries after the Close Date:

- Corporate Controller
- Assistant Controller
- Designated close-team members (currently: J. Smith and M. Williams, as maintained in the system access list).

Any entry posted after the Close Date by an individual not on the approved list is an automatic policy violation and will be flagged by the system for investigation.

### 3.3 Materiality Threshold

- Post-close adjustments below $5,000 that are corrections of prior-period accruals may be posted with email approval from the Assistant Controller.
- Post-close adjustments of $5,000 or above require a signed memo approved by the Corporate Controller.
- Post-close adjustments of $100,000 or above require CFO notification.

### 3.4 Documentation

All post-close entries must include:

1. A reference to the approval email or signed memo.
2. An explanation of why the entry could not be made before the Close Date.
3. The specific period the entry relates to.

## 4. Period Lock Controls

The ERP system is configured to automatically lock the general ledger for a given period 24 hours after the Close Date. The system administrator may reopen a period only upon written request from the Controller, and must log the reopening with a reason code.

## 5. Monitoring

Internal Audit reviews all post-close entries quarterly. Entries posted after the Close Date are included in the scope of the annual journal entry testing program. Patterns of late entries by department or individual are reported to the CFO.

## 6. Violations

Unauthorized post-close entries will be reversed and re-posted in the correct period. Repeat violations may result in removal of posting access and escalation to the department head.
