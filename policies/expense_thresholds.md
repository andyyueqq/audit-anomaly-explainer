# Expense Account Thresholds and Monitoring Policy

**Policy Number:** FIN-EX-003
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Last Revised:** October 20, 2024
**Owner:** Accounting Manager

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes monitoring thresholds for expense accounts to detect unusual or potentially erroneous postings. It defines the expected ranges for common expense categories and the escalation procedures when thresholds are exceeded.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all operating expense accounts in the general ledger (account range 5000–5999). Capital expenditures, cost of goods sold, and non-operating items are governed by separate policies.

## 4. Threshold Exceedance Procedures

When a single journal entry exceeds the threshold for its account:

### 4.1 Automatic Flag

The data-analytics monitoring system (currently Alteryx-based) will flag any entry where the amount exceeds 3x the monthly average for the account. These flags are generated weekly and distributed to the Internal Audit team.

### 4.2 Review Process

1. The preparer must provide written justification for the amount.
2. The reviewer identified in the threshold table must approve the entry with documented rationale.
3. If the entry exceeds 10x the monthly average, the Assistant Controller must also review and approve.

### 4.3 Acceptable Explanations

The following are examples of acceptable explanations for threshold exceedances:

- Annual or semi-annual payments (e.g., annual insurance premium, annual software license).
- One-time project costs with an approved budget reference.
- Catch-up accruals for prior periods with Controller approval.
- Reclassifications from other accounts with supporting documentation.

The following are NOT acceptable explanations:

- "Per management request" without further detail.
- "Year-end adjustment" without specifying the nature of the adjustment.
- "Correction" without identifying the original error.

## 5. Cumulative Monitoring

In addition to single-entry thresholds, the monthly cumulative total for each expense account is monitored. If the cumulative monthly total for any account exceeds 150% of the expected monthly average, a variance analysis must be prepared by the account owner and reviewed by the Accounting Manager.

## 6. Reporting

A monthly threshold exceedance report is prepared by the Financial Reporting team and shared with the Controller and Internal Audit. The report includes all flagged entries, the review status, and any unresolved items.
