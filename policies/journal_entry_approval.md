# Journal Entry Approval Policy

**Policy Number:** FIN-AP-001
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Last Revised:** December 15, 2024
**Owner:** Corporate Controller

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the requirements for the preparation, review, and approval of all journal entries recorded in the general ledger. It is designed to ensure the accuracy and completeness of financial records and to maintain adequate segregation of duties.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all manual journal entries, recurring journal entries, and adjusting entries recorded in the company's general ledger system. Automated system-generated entries (e.g., payroll, accounts payable batch postings) are excluded from the manual approval requirement but are subject to periodic review.

## 3. Approval Thresholds

All manual journal entries require approval before posting. The required approval level depends on the dollar amount of the entry:

- **Entries up to $10,000:** Approved by a Staff Accountant or above.
- **Entries from $10,001 to $50,000:** Approved by a Senior Accountant or Accounting Manager.
- **Entries from $50,001 to $200,000:** Approved by the Accounting Manager or Assistant Controller.
- **Entries exceeding $200,000:** Approved by the Corporate Controller or CFO.

No individual may approve their own journal entry. The preparer and approver must be different individuals.

## 4. Documentation Requirements

Every manual journal entry must include:

1. A clear and specific description of the business purpose.
2. Supporting documentation or a reference to the source document (invoice, contract, calculation workpaper).
3. The account codes for all debits and credits.
4. The name of the preparer and the date of preparation.
5. The name of the approver and the date of approval.

Entries with descriptions such as "miscellaneous," "adjustment," or "correction" without further explanation are not acceptable and must be rejected by the approver.

## 5. Segregation of Duties

The following segregation requirements apply:

- The individual who prepares a journal entry may not also approve it.
- The individual who approves a journal entry may not also post it, unless system controls enforce the approval before posting.
- Access to create and post journal entries must be restricted based on job role and reviewed quarterly by IT and the Controller's office.

## 6. Unusual or Non-Routine Entries

Journal entries that meet any of the following criteria are classified as non-routine and require enhanced review:

- Entries with round-dollar amounts exceeding $50,000.
- Entries that debit revenue accounts or credit expense accounts (i.e., entries that reduce revenue or reduce expenses).
- Entries posted by individuals outside the Finance department.
- Entries with no supporting documentation attached at the time of posting.
- Entries involving related-party transactions.

Non-routine entries must be reviewed by the Accounting Manager or above, regardless of the dollar amount, and must include a written explanation of the business rationale.

## 7. Violations

Failure to comply with this policy may result in:

- Reversal of the non-compliant journal entry.
- A formal exception report filed with Internal Audit.
- Disciplinary action for repeat violations.

## 8. Review Cycle

This policy is reviewed annually by the Corporate Controller and approved by the CFO. Any changes to approval thresholds or documentation requirements must be communicated to all Finance personnel within 10 business days of the effective date.
