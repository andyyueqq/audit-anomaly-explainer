# Chart of Accounts Usage and Pairing Guidelines

**Policy Number:** FIN-CA-005
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Last Revised:** August 10, 2024
**Owner:** Corporate Controller

## 1. Purpose

This policy provides guidance on the proper use of general-ledger accounts and defines expected (normal) and unusual (abnormal) account pairings in journal entries. It is intended to help preparers select the correct accounts and to help reviewers identify potentially erroneous or fraudulent entries.

## 2. Account Classification

The chart of accounts is organized into the following ranges:

| Range       | Category              | Normal Balance |
|------------|----------------------|----------------|
| 1000–1999  | Assets               | Debit          |
| 2000–2999  | Liabilities          | Credit         |
| 3000–3999  | Equity               | Credit         |
| 4000–4999  | Revenue              | Credit         |
| 5000–5999  | Operating Expenses   | Debit          |

## 3. Normal Account Pairings

The following are examples of standard, expected journal entry pairings:

### 3.1 Expense Recognition
- **Debit:** Expense account (5000s) / **Credit:** Cash (1010) or Accounts Payable (2010) or Accrued Liabilities (2200).
- Example: Recording a consulting invoice — Debit 5600 Consulting Expense, Credit 2010 Accounts Payable.

### 3.2 Revenue Recognition
- **Debit:** Cash (1010) or Accounts Receivable (1200) / **Credit:** Revenue account (4000s).
- Example: Recording a customer payment — Debit 1010 Cash, Credit 4000 Revenue.

### 3.3 Asset Transactions
- **Debit:** Asset account / **Credit:** Cash or Accounts Payable.
- Example: Purchasing equipment — Debit 1500 Fixed Assets, Credit 1010 Cash.

### 3.4 Liability Payments
- **Debit:** Liability account / **Credit:** Cash.
- Example: Paying a vendor — Debit 2010 Accounts Payable, Credit 1010 Cash.

## 4. Unusual Account Pairings

The following pairings are considered unusual and require enhanced scrutiny. Entries using these pairings must include a detailed explanation and may trigger an Internal Audit review.

### 4.1 Revenue Debits
- **Debit to a Revenue account (4000s)** paired with any credit is unusual because it reduces recognized revenue. Legitimate reasons include sales returns, billing corrections, or reclassifications between revenue streams. Any revenue debit exceeding $5,000 requires Accounting Manager approval.

### 4.2 Expense Credits
- **Credit to an Expense account (5000s)** is unusual because it reduces recognized expenses. Legitimate reasons include vendor refunds, insurance reimbursements, or reclassifications. The original expense entry must be referenced.

### 4.3 Revenue Paired with Assets or Prepaid
- **Debit to Revenue (4000s) / Credit to Prepaid Expenses (1300)** or other asset accounts is highly unusual. This combination could indicate improper deferral of recognized revenue or a posting error. Such entries require Assistant Controller review and a written memo explaining the business rationale.

### 4.4 Equity Paired with Expenses
- **Debit to Equity (3000s) / Credit to Expense accounts (5000s)** is unusual. This could indicate an attempt to reclassify expenses as equity adjustments. Entries with this pairing require Corporate Controller approval.

### 4.5 Cross-Category Entries Without Clear Business Purpose
- Any entry that pairs accounts from non-adjacent categories (e.g., Fixed Assets with Revenue, Deferred Revenue with Inventory) without a clear, documented business reason should be flagged for review. Common legitimate scenarios include asset disposals (Fixed Assets to Revenue/Gain) and prepaid amortization (Prepaid to Expense).

## 5. Prohibited Pairings

The following pairings are prohibited without explicit Controller or CFO approval:

- Debit to Retained Earnings (3000) paired with any expense credit — this may circumvent the income statement.
- Any entry that simultaneously increases an asset and increases revenue without a corresponding customer transaction.
- Entries that offset each other across periods (e.g., a debit in December reversed by a credit in January with no underlying economic event).

## 6. Monitoring

The Internal Audit team maintains an automated report that flags all journal entries with unusual account pairings as defined in Section 4. This report is generated monthly and reviewed by the Accounting Manager and Internal Audit lead.

## 7. New Account Requests

New general-ledger accounts may only be created by the Corporate Controller or designated team members. Requests must include the account name, range, normal balance, expected pairings, and business justification.
